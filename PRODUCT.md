# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Cubestats is primarily for competitive speedcubers who want to inspect official WCA performance history with enough detail to spot progress, plateaus, strengths, weaknesses, and head-to-head context. They are usually entering a WCA ID with a concrete question in mind: how a competitor has improved, which events are strongest, how results compare across time, or how two competitors stack up.

## Product Purpose

Cubestats turns official WCA result data into a focused analytics workspace: personal-best progression, average history, event statistics, all-around profiles, activity patterns, milestones, raw round results, and competitor comparison. Success means a speedcuber can move from a WCA ID to a trustworthy read of performance history without manual spreadsheet work or noisy dashboard friction.

## Brand Personality

Precise, fast, specialist. The interface should feel like a serious analysis tool made for people who understand WCA competition data, while still being approachable for a speedcuber checking their own history for the first time.

## Anti-references

Cubestats should not feel like a generic corporate dashboard, a KPI reporting suite, a gamified toy, or obvious AI slop. Avoid decorative analytics cliches, inflated SaaS hero patterns, generic card grids, vague "insights" language, and visual choices that make official competition data feel less credible.

## Design Principles

- Lead with the competitor's task: lookup, compare, inspect, and drill into events should stay faster than presentation.
- Treat WCA data as precise evidence: ranks, dates, solves, averages, and DNF markers need clear hierarchy and careful formatting.
- Preserve analytical density without clutter: compact views are useful, but scanning should not require deciphering the interface.
- Use familiarity where it builds trust: standard controls, tables, tabs, segmented filters, and chart conventions should behave predictably.
- Keep personality in the details: speedcubing-specific terminology, examples, and microcopy should carry the voice more than decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for text contrast and interactive states. Charts and heatmaps should not rely on color alone where the value matters; tooltips, labels, table data, or alternate text should preserve meaning. Reduced-motion preferences should be respected, and dense tables or charts should remain navigable on small screens without hiding essential data.
